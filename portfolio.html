Crown Heights: Who Landmarks the Landmarks?

Renaissance chateaux and Medieval castles inspired the picturesque facades of thousands of Crown Heights rowhouses, which housed some of Brooklyn’s wealthiest professionals in the late 1800s. A century of transformation followed—an influx of African-American southerners fleeing Jim Crow, two World Wars, suburbanization and white flight, the formation of communities of Caribbean immigrants and Chabad-Lubavitch Hasidim and their accompanying racial tension, decades of crime and urban decay followed by renewal and contemporary gentrification—but much of the old architecture remains well-preserved.
